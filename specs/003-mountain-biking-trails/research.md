## Design Decisions

### D1: Trail System Tier Structure

**Decision**: Three tiers for trail systems:
- **Primary**: Arrowhead Trails (default recommendation)
- **Conditional**: Summit Bechtel Reserve (profile fully, but flag access uncertainty)
- **Supplementary**: Babcock State Park (light ride / scenic option)

**Rationale**: Honest tiering prevents the group from planning around an inaccessible system while still documenting the full range of options.

### D2: February Viability Rating Scale

**Decision**: Use a 4-level scale matching the bouldering rain resilience pattern:
- **Good**: Reliably rideable most February days with appropriate timing
- **Fair**: Rideable on good days, check conditions morning-of
- **Poor**: Rarely rideable in February, high risk of closures/damage
- **Uncertain**: Access or conditions cannot be confirmed â€” verify before trip

**Rationale**: Consistent with the bouldering area ratings. "Uncertain" is new â€” needed for Summit Bechtel where the issue is access, not just conditions.

### D3: Bike Rental Section Placement

**Decision**: Include rental info in `trails/README.md` overview rather than a separate file. The data is small (3-4 shops) and belongs in the decision-making flow.

**Rationale**: A separate file would be overkill for 3-4 shop entries. Riders deciding whether to ride need rental info alongside trail options.

### D4: Bouldering Pairing Section Placement

**Decision**: Include pairings in `trails/README.md` as a dedicated section, not a separate file.

**Rationale**: Pairings are a planning tool that belongs alongside the trail overview. Creating a separate file fragments the decision flow.
